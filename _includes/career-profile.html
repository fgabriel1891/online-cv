About me

I have been always passionate about biodiversity. I completed my BSc. degree in Biology at Universidad San Francisco de Quito (USFQ) with a minor in Environmental Management in 2013. Doing my degree in Ecuador, allowed my to experience biodiversity at close proximity and participate in a variety of biodiversity-related projects ranging from microbes to whales. However, for my final BSc. research project I decided to study the effects of an altitudinal gradient on the community strucuture of araneid spiders in the northern Andes of Ecuador. After completing my Bachelor's degree, I worked first as a freelance consultant for different conservation - related projects for the PNUD and the Ecuadorian Ministry of Environment. After this short experience in the public sector, I went back to USFQ to work in the Terrestrial Zoology Lab. There, amongst other tasks, I contributed to the Red List Assesment of endemic ecuadorian reptiles, birds and amphibian species. As I had decided to keep on with an academic career I started a MSc. in Biological Sciences, track Ecology and Evolution at the University of Amsterdam (UvA) in 2015. At UvA I joined to the Theorethical Ecology Group where I concentrate my research efforts in the study of species interaction networks, focusing in palm - frugivore seed dispersal interactions. Additionally, I have gained an interest in the use of programming and computational tools for research in Ecology.
